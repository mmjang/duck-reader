The Terrible Toll of 76 Autoimmune Diseases

This list shows how common each disorder is, which body parts are stricken, and the illnesses’ tendency to afflict women

  • By Maddie Bender, Jen Christiansen, Miriam Quick

The idea that organisms might attack themselves with immune with immune systems that evolved to defend them from diseases in the outside world made little sense to immunologist Paul Ehrlich. In 1901 the future Nobel laureate dismissed such a theory—he called it “horror autotoxicus”—as farfetched. Today patients and physicians know that autoimmune diseases are all too real. There are roughly 80 autoimmune conditions that affect millions of people worldwide. The variety and numbers shown in the chart on the next two pages are daunting.

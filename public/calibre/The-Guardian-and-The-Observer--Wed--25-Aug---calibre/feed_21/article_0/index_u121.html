What is the point of Arsenal? – Football Weekly

Max Rushden is joined by Barry Glendenning, Mark Langdon and Faye Carruthers to review the weekend action in the Premier League

How to listen to podcasts: everything you need to know

Presented by Max Rushden with Barry Glendenning, Faye Carruthers and Mark Langdon

Mon 23 Aug 2021 13.48 BST

Rate, review, share on Apple Podcasts, Soundcloud, Audioboom, Mixcloud, Acast and Stitcher, and join the conversation on Facebook, Twitter and email.

Romelu Lukaku shines on his second debut for Chelsea while we all repeat the question - what’s the point of Arsenal?

Spurs sneak past Wolves with Harry Kane back on the pitch as a sub, Brighton show some love for the returning Shane Duffy and at Liverpool, a lovely Sadio Mané goal helps to beat Burnley at Anfield, something the Reds failed to do last season.

Jürgen Klopp and Ole Gunnar Solskjær question the new policy of ‘letting the game flow’, suggesting the game is descending into bedlam; with the Manchester United man suggesting it is shifting “from one extreme, like basketball last season into rugby now”.

Elsewhere in Mark Langdon’s European roundup, lawless anarchy erupts in France, there is more Cristiano Ronaldo disquiet, and we discuss Gareth Bale’s revealing Guardian Q&A.
